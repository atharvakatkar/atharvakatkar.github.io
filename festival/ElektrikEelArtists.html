OUR LINEUP

21 Savage, born Shéyaa Bin Abraham-Joseph in 1992, is a British-American rapper and songwriter. Known for his distinct Atlanta-based trap sound, he gained fame for introspective lyrics detailing his experiences growing up in poverty and his involvement in street life. His music often touches on social issues and personal struggles.

Ariana Grande, born in 1993, is an American singer, songwriter, and actress. Rising to prominence through her role in the TV show "Victorious," she transitioned into a successful music career with her powerful vocal range and pop-R&B fusion. Grande's chart-topping hits and distinctive style have established her as a global pop icon.

Carti likely refers to Playboi Carti, a rapper born Jordan Terrell Carter. Emerging from Atlanta's rap scene, he gained popularity for his unique delivery, ad-libs, and experimental trap sound. His album "Die Lit" and the viral hit "Magnolia" are notable successes. Carti is known for blending fashion and music, influencing modern hip-hop culture.

DJ Snake, born William Sami Étienne Grigahcine, is a French DJ, record producer, and songwriter. He's recognized for his electronic, dancehall, and trap-influenced music. His breakout hit "Turn Down for What" marked his international breakthrough. DJ Snake's collaborations with top artists and energetic performances have solidified his status as a prominent figure in the electronic music scene.

Doja Cat, born Amala Ratna Zandile Dlamin, is an American singer, rapper, and songwriter. Rising to fame with her viral hit "Mooo!" and the album "Hot Pink," she's known for her genre-blending sound that combines pop, hip-hop, and R&B. Doja Cat's creativity, catchy tunes, and engaging online presence have propelled her to pop stardom.

Future, born Nayvadius Wilburn, is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. Hailing from Atlanta, he's a prominent figure in hip-hop, known for his distinctive autotuned style and introspective lyrics. His albums like "DS2" and "Hndrxx" have received acclaim, showcasing his ability to deliver both trap anthems and emotive tracks, solidifying his influence on the genre.

Gesaffelstein, born Mike Lévy, is a French electronic music producer and DJ. He gained recognition for his dark, industrial sound and visually captivating live performances. Known for blending techno and electronic elements, Gesaffelstein has released critically acclaimed albums like "Aleph" and has collaborated with artists like The Weeknd. His work pushes the boundaries of electronic music.

Metro Boomin, born Leland Tyler Wayne, is an American record producer and DJ. He's renowned for shaping the sound of modern hip-hop and trap music. Collaborating with prominent artists like Future, Drake, and Migos, he's produced numerous chart-topping hits. Metro Boomin's signature tag "Metro Boomin want some more, nigga!" is iconic in the genre.

Nicki Minaj, born Onika Tanya Maraj, is a Trinidadian-American rapper, singer, and songwriter. She's celebrated for her dynamic flow, bold fashion, and energetic performances. Minaj's albums, like "Pink Friday" and "The Pinkprint," have produced chart-topping hits. She's recognized for her versatility in both rap and pop, and her impact on modern female representation in hip-hop.

Moodymann, whose real name is Kenny Dixon Jr., is an American electronic music producer and DJ. Hailing from Detroit, he's a significant figure in the house and techno music scene. Moodymann's music often blends soul, funk, and jazz influences, creating a unique and groovy sound. He's revered for his enigmatic persona and contributions to the evolution of electronic music.

Post Malone, born Austin Richard Post, is an American singer, rapper, and songwriter. Rising to fame with hits like "White Iverson," he's known for blending genres like hip-hop, pop, and rock in his music. His albums, including "Stoney" and "Beerbongs & Bentleys," have achieved massive commercial success. Post Malone's unique voice and versatile style have solidified his place as a modern pop superstar.

Tyler, the Creator, born Tyler Gregory Okonma, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and producer. Emerging from the hip-hop collective Odd Future, he's known for his creative and boundary-pushing approach to music and visuals. His albums, like "Goblin" and "IGOR," showcase his evolution from controversial lyrics to introspective themes, earning him critical acclaim and a dedicated fanbase.

The Weeknd, born Abel Makkonen Tesfaye, is a Canadian singer, songwriter, and record producer. Rising to prominence with his mixtapes, he's known for his smooth R&B vocals, moody production, and introspective lyrics. Albums like "Trilogy," "Beauty Behind the Madness," and "After Hours" have propelled him to global stardom. The Weeknd's genre-blurring sound and distinctive visuals have left an indelible mark on modern pop and R&B.

Paul Kalkbrenner, born on June 11, 1977, is a renowned German electronic music producer and DJ. With his distinctive blend of techno and melodic elements, Kalkbrenner has left an indelible mark on the global electronic music scene. His pioneering sound and captivating performances continue to inspire and influence music enthusiasts worldwide.

Jay-Z, born Shawn Corey Carter, is an American rapper, songwriter, record producer, and entrepreneur. He's one of the most influential figures in hip-hop, known for his intricate lyrics, storytelling prowess, and entrepreneurial ventures. Albums like "The Blueprint" and "4:44" showcase his evolution and impact on the genre. Jay-Z's success extends beyond music, with business ventures in fashion, sports, and philanthropy.
